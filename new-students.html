Getting Started at UWEC

üè† Finding Housing

UWEC offers residence halls for students. First-year students should contact the Residence Life office early to secure on-campus housing. For off-campus options, check out local rental agencies.

üç¥ Dining Options

The Student Center has a dining hall with various meal plans. There are also restaurants around campus and in the Eau Claire area. Don't miss exploring local restaurants!

üöå Transportation

UWEC offers free bus passes for students. The Eau Claire Transit System can help you navigate the city. Many students also bike or walk around campus during nice weather.

Essential Tips for International Students

‚úì Documentation

Always keep your passport, visa, I-20 or DS-160 forms, and insurance documents in a safe place. Make copies and store them separately.

‚úì Social Security Number (SSN)

While not always required, you may need an SSN for certain jobs and services. Visit the SSA office with your employment authorization letter and passport.

‚úì Part-Time Work

As an international student, you can typically work up to 20 hours per week on campus or 23 hours off-campus during the academic term. Check with the International Office for details.

‚úì Stay Connected

Join CSSA and other student organizations! Building community helps you adjust faster and makes your college experience more enriching.

‚úì Health Insurance

Make sure you have health insurance. UWEC requires all students to have coverage. International students may be able to purchase the school's plan.

‚úì Tax Filing

International students may have special tax considerations. Consult with the International Office or a tax professional about your obligations.
